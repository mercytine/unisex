Social controversy over Unisex

关于unisex，人们有许多不同的看法。我们从中略知一二，得出自己的判断。
There are many different opinions about UNISEX. We learn a thing or two from them and make our own judgments.

How it Work

 「时尚的未来不是没有性别的标签，而是没有性别的消费者」。

"The future of fashion is not genderless labels, but genderless consumers."


在新鲜事物出现之初，无例外的会有人感到困惑和不安，性别无差异化概念更是在极为基本的层面上引起了社会认知的混乱，引发反感、讨论可想而知。但“正如中世纪社会通过上帝和魔鬼来建立平衡，我们的社会是通过消费及对其的揭示来建立平衡（鲍德里亚《消费社会》）”，当人们意识到个体能够脱离性别而存在，“有性别“和”无性别“的对立便无法再消失。

At the beginning of something new, there is no exception that some people will feel confused and uneasy. The concept of gender non-differentiation even causes confusion in social cognition at a very basic level, which can be imagined as causing antipathy and discussion. But "just as the medieval society established its balance through God and the devil, our society established its balance through consumption and its revelation (Baudrillard's Consumer Society)", when people realize that individuals can exist without gender, the opposition between "gender" and "non-gender" can no longer disappear.

Clothing is not always gender-specific

我们遇到别人时，总会习惯性地先去判断对方的性别，美国历史学家Annie Woolhouse却说事实并非如此，“我们最先注意到的是他人心理性别传达出的外在表现，只是假设这样的表现是他们生理性别的准确表达而已”。着装作为个人外在表现的首要工具，自然会和性别产生联系，但服装并非一直有着明显的性别标识。而透过含义丰富的着装，时尚从来都是颠覆性别身份的一项有力工具。
“虽然社会规则对于男女性别的边界有着严格的道德和法律界限，但艺术却得以相对自由地承载人们对于性别模糊的探索与想象。”

Annie Woolhouse, an American historian, said that this is not the case. "The first thing we notice is the external expression of others' psychological gender, only assuming that this expression is an accurate expression of their biological gender." As the primary tool of personal external expression, clothing will naturally be associated with gender, but clothing does not always have an obvious gender identity. And fashion has always been a powerful tool for subverting gender identity through meaningful clothing.
"Although social rules impose strict moral and legal boundaries on gender, art is relatively free to carry people's exploration and imagination of gender ambiguity."

 “虽然社会规则对于男女性别的边界有着严格的道德和法律界限，但艺术却得以相对自由地承载人们对于性别模糊的探索与想象。”
"Although social rules impose strict moral and legal boundaries on gender, art is relatively free to carry people's exploration and imagination of gender ambiguity."

不要再以为无性别的审美潮流仍停留在T台之上，大众文化其实已经对无性别主义开始广泛的接受，不再局限于小众人群之中。而我们迎来的，也许不只是一场审美革命，性别可能会成为继年龄之后的第二个个人隐私，它只是每个人的个人选择，而不会被规则。 
Don't think that gender-neutral aesthetic trends are still on the catwalk. Popular culture has begun to embrace asexualism, no longer confined to the minority. What we are about to usher in, however, may be more than an aesthetic revolution. Gender may become a second personal privacy after age, which is only a personal choice for each individual and will not be governed by rules.
  • 对国内而言：虽然目前国内的性别文化依旧是一道桎梏，不论是女性还是男性在做出自己喜好的选择时，依旧容易承受他人的评头论足，但国内的设计师们也在努力地去推动这一理念的达成，不论是江南布衣旗下的 REVERB 还是已经颇有名气 bosie，还是2020年刚刚成军的 Oinkoink。 他们正在向国内的消费者们传递一种声音： 这样去穿衣服才是真正的自由。
  • Domestically: Although gender culture in China is still a constraint, both women and men are still vulnerable to criticism when making their own choices, Chinese designers are also working hard to promote this concept, from Reverb of Jiangnan Cloi to Bosie, which is already famous. Or Oinkoink, which will be launched in 2020. They are sending a message to domestic consumers that this is the way to wear clothes that is the real freedom.
